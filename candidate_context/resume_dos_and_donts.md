# Resume Do's and Don'ts

## Do

- Keep claims evidence-based.
- Prefer clear technical language over exaggerated marketing language.
- Emphasize computational chemistry, machine learning, scientific software, and open-source engineering when relevant.
- Tailor the summary and skills section to the job description.
- Reorder bullets to prioritize the most relevant experience.
- Preserve factual dates, institutions, titles, and project names.
- Flag weak matches instead of pretending they are strong matches.

## Don't

- Do not invent employers, titles, dates, publications, awards, metrics, or responsibilities.
- Do not add tools just because they appear in the job description.
- Do not claim production ownership unless supported.
- Do not overstate leadership or management experience.
- Do not convert research prototypes into deployed products unless evidence supports that.
- Do not remove important research context unless the target role clearly requires a shorter industry framing.
- Do not use vague filler such as “results-driven,” “dynamic,” or “passionate professional.
- Do not use 'em dash', use 'en dash'.
